---

I operate largely on the assumption that things will get easier with time and experience. This is, empirically, sometimes true and sometimes wildly not true. Below is an honest accounting.

---

**Waking up before 8am**

I have been trying to become a morning person for approximately twelve years. I have read the books. I have set the alarms — multiple alarms, with increasingly aggressive names like "SERIOUSLY GET UP" and "YOU SAID YOU WOULD." I have tried going to bed earlier, which just means I lie awake for longer. Morning remains my adversary. We have reached a détente but not a friendship.

**Making phone calls to strangers**

The task: call to make a dentist appointment. My brain's assessment of this task: *deeply unpleasant, possibly avoidable, let's think about it tomorrow.* I have had the same dentist for eight years. I still feel mild dread every time I have to call them. This has not improved.

**Knowing when a piece of work is actually done**

This one felt like it would get better with experience and the development of taste. Instead, experience and taste have made it worse. I now know more ways in which something could be improved, which means I can find more reasons to keep fiddling. I have shipped things I later thought were too early. I have also polished things until they were just... differently imperfect. There is no answer here. There is only the deadline.

**Grocery shopping without a list**

I go in for four things. I come out with seventeen things. The four things are not among them. This has happened more times than I can count. I have given up understanding it and moved to acceptance.

**Letting go of a good comeback I thought of too late**

The conversation ended hours ago. The moment is gone. The comment I should have made still lives in my head, fully formed, sharp, and completely useless. I replay conversations with better versions of myself in them. This is not a productive use of my brain. My brain doesn't care.

---

Things that *did* get easier, for balance:

- Saying "I don't know"
- Asking for help
- Deciding not to go to events I don't want to go to
- Being wrong about things
- Ordering confidently at restaurants, even the fancy ones

Progress is not linear. But it is progress.

Happy new year.
